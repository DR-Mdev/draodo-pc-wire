Who we work with

Built for international companies that take Japan seriously.

Dorado works best with growing companies that want practical support, realistic sequencing, and structured external help as they explore or advance Japan entry.

Best-fit profile

The companies we tend to help most.

Dorado is built for companies with a real commitment to Japan — not passing curiosity. The fit works best when there is genuine capacity to invest in structured exploration.

  • Companies with stable core operations

    Your core business is established in your home market and you have the capacity to invest time and attention in Japan exploration alongside it.

  • Teams with limited local Japan visibility

    You have interest in Japan but limited existing networks, local knowledge, or execution capacity on the ground.

  • Founders or leadership teams who want structure

    You want an external partner who helps design the work, not just execute tasks. Shared priorities and visibility matter to you.

  • Companies that want to reduce avoidable mistakes

    You understand that getting the operating model right before scaling commitment matters more than moving fast before you are ready.

Context we typically work in

Common starting situations

Where most conversations begin.

Companies come to Dorado from different starting points. These are the situations that tend to show up most often at the beginning of the work.

01

You are interested in Japan but unsure what the right first step is. You have done internal research but do not yet have a clear operating picture.

02

You need a clearer view of demand, channels, or possible partner routes before you can make a confident internal decision about how to proceed.

03

You want to pressure-test assumptions before allocating a larger budget or committing to local hiring or incorporation.

04

You have some traction or early interest already but no structured operating rhythm to turn that into consistent, trackable progress.

05

You need support turning discussions into actual next steps — defining priorities, preparing materials, coordinating outreach, or following through on open threads.

06

You have tried moving forward independently but found that without local knowledge or structured external support, progress has been slower than expected.

Fit check

How to tell if this is likely to work.

The working relationship matters as much as the service itself. This is who the model is designed for — and who it is probably not designed for.

Good fit

Companies that benefit from working this way

  • You want clarity before making heavy commitments in Japan
  • You value transparent communication and realistic expectations about uncertainty
  • You are willing to share enough internal context for the work to be useful
  • You want support across both structured thinking and practical execution
  • You understand that good market entry work is iterative and decision-driven

Probably not a fit

Situations where this model is less likely to work

  • You need guaranteed commercial outcomes within a short fixed timeframe
  • You only want ad hoc execution with no shared structure or decision-making
  • You prefer not to share relevant business context or internal constraints
  • You want large-scale execution immediately without prior validation
  • You are looking for a low-cost vendor to follow instructions without discussion
